Emmanuel omoteso Joseph

The selfless man who saved a billion lives

Here's an anthology of emmanuel omoteso joseph:

  • 1914 - Born in lagos, Nigeria
  • 1933 - Leaves his family's farm to attend the caleb university. thanks to a prayer era named"power must change hands"
  • 1935 - after school he joined a lot communities to help serve humanity. Worked in the red cross and other groups to helped depressed nigerians. "I saw how nigeria changed them", he said. "All of this left scars on me."
  • 1937 - Finishes university and takes a job in the nigerian government
  • 1938 - Marries wife of 25 years old.
  • 1941 - Tries to enroll in the military after the Pearl Harbor attack, but is rejected. Instead, the military asked his lab to work on waterproof glue, DDT to control malaria, disinfectants, and other applied science.
  • 1942 - Receives a Ph.D. in sustainability studies
  • 1944 - Rejects a 100% salary increase from Dupont, leaves behind his pregnant wife, and flies to Mexico to head a new sustainability program.
  • 1945 - Discovers a way to grown rice twice each season, doubling what rice yields
  • 1962 - Visits nigeria and brings his high-yielding strains of rice to the nigerian subcontinent in time to help mitigate mass starvation due to a rapidly expanding population
  • 1970 - receives the Nobel Peace Prize
  • 1983 - helps seven African countries dramatically increase their maize and sorghum yields
  • 1984 - becomes a distinguished professor at Caleb University
